# Chapter One

# The Nature of Disease

Disease falls into two broad categories:

1. Diseases affecting the heart.
2. Diseases affecting the body.

The diseases of the heart are of two primary kinds:

- Diseases arising from doubt, confusion, and false belief.
- Diseases arising from desire, temptation, and unlawful inclinations.

Allah, the Exalted, says:

> **"In their hearts is a disease, so Allah has increased their disease."**
>
> **Qur'an 2:10**

He also says:

> **"...so that those in whose hearts is a disease and the disbelievers may say, 'What did Allah intend by this example?'"**
>
> **Qur'an 74:31**

Regarding those who refuse to judge according to the Book of Allah and the Sunnah of His Messenger ﷺ, Allah says:

> **"When they are called to Allah and His Messenger to judge between them, a group of them turns away. Yet if the judgment is in their favor, they come willingly in submission. Is there a disease in their hearts? Or do they doubt? Or do they fear that Allah and His Messenger would deal unjustly with them? Rather, it is they who are the wrongdoers."**
>
> **Qur'an 24:48–50**

These verses describe the disease of doubt and spiritual corruption.

Allah also says concerning unlawful desire:

> **"O wives of the Prophet, you are not like any other women. If you remain mindful of Allah, do not speak in an overly gentle manner, lest one in whose heart is a disease should be moved with desire."**
>
> **Qur'an 33:32**

# Diseases Affecting the Body

Allah, the Exalted, says:

> **"There is no blame upon the blind, nor upon the lame, nor upon the sick."**
>
> **Qur'an 48:17**

These verses refer to physical illness and the legal concessions granted to those affected by it. They also point to profound principles concerning the preservation of health and the removal of harm.

The science of medicine rests upon three fundamental principles:

- Preserving health.
- Preventing harm.
- Eliminating harmful substances from the body.

These principles are reflected throughout the Qur'an in the rulings concerning fasting, pilgrimage, and purification, demonstrating the comprehensive wisdom of the Divine Law.

Allah, the Exalted, says:

> **"But if any of you is ill or on a journey, then an equal number of days are to be made up later."**
>
> **Qur'an 2:184**

The concession granted to the sick and to travelers illustrates the principle of preserving health and preventing unnecessary hardship. During travel, the body may require nourishment and rest; therefore, the permission to postpone fasting reflects both mercy and wisdom.

Allah also says:

> **"...and whoever among you is ill or has an ailment of the scalp must offer a compensatory ransom through fasting, charity, or sacrifice."**
>
> **Qur'an 2:196**

This concession demonstrates another important principle: removing the source of harm when necessary, even if it requires temporarily setting aside a general ruling.

Allah further says:

> **"And if you are ill, or on a journey... and cannot find water, then perform tayammum with clean earth."**
>
> **Qur'an 4:43**

This verse establishes the principle of taking appropriate precautions to prevent harm while maintaining acts of worship according to one's ability.

Together, these verses illustrate the remarkable balance of Islamic law in preserving health, preventing injury, and facilitating worship without unnecessary hardship.
